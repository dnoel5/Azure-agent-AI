3. CASA TESTING CENTER RULES
==================================================

General testing rules:
- Students must register biometric information before the exam date if required and not previously completed.
- Biometric registration is a one-time process.
- Students must bring valid identification for biometric registration and check-in.
- Acceptable ID may include:
  - UH Cougar Card
  - state-issued ID
  - passport
  - digital Cougar ID from the UH Go app
- A photo ID is required to check in.
- Exams are by reservation only.
- Students must schedule their exam in advance using their Courseware/CCS account.
- Check-in starts 20 minutes before the scheduled exam time.
- Students may check in no later than 15 minutes after the reservation time, based on the CASA front desk wall clock.
- No food or drinks allowed.
- Exams begin on the hour.
- There is no standby testing.
- Only items essential to testing are allowed at the workstation.
- Personal belongings must be left at the check-in desk.
- Prohibited personal items may include:
  - backpacks
  - purses
  - notebooks
  - calculators unless specifically allowed
  - pagers
  - cell phones
  - recording devices
  - photography equipment
- Raise your hand for assistance.
- Return all testing materials and scratch paper at checkout.
- CASA is not responsible for lost, misplaced, or damaged personal belongings.

Weapons policy:
Students in CASA testing and tutoring centers may not possess items that can be used as weapons, including but not limited to:
- handguns
- firearms
- ammunition
- fireworks
- pellet guns
- paintball guns
- BB guns
- knives
- clubs
- explosive or noxious materials

What to bring:
- Photo ID
- Pen or pencil if instructed
- Other allowed items only if required by the course or testing instructions

What is provided:
- Scratch paper

Cell phone rule:
- Cell phones must be completely turned off or placed on silent as directed; they are not permitted for use in the testing area.

==================================================

Who needs to register with CASA?
Any student who is required to take an exam at the CASA Testing Center must register with CASA.

6. SCHEDULING AND RESERVING EXAMS
==================================================

How do students schedule an exam?
Go to:
https://ccs.casa.uh.edu/canvas2.html

Log in with Cougarnet credentials and make a reservation.

When does scheduling open?
- Spring and Fall: two weeks before the exam date
- Summer and mini semesters: one week before the exam date

Can students reschedule exams?
Yes, depending on seat availability and until the testing window closes.

If a reservation is forgotten:
Students should schedule as soon as possible before the actual exam date/time. Early scheduling is strongly recommended because seats may fill.

If a scheduled exam does not appear in the account:
Students are responsible for making sure the reservation was properly saved before leaving the scheduler.

7. MISSED EXAMS
==================================================

What if a student misses an exam?
The student must check the online scheduler for another available testing time.

CASA staff do not reschedule missed exams for students.

What if the last available slot is missed?
The student must contact the instructor.
Instructor policy determines what happens next.

9. TUTORING FAQ
==================================================

Homework:
- Undergraduate math homework may use EMCF (Electronic Multiple Choice Form).
- Students can access EMCF through their Courseware account.

Can homework be turned in early or late?
No. Students must submit by the deadline listed in the syllabus.

What does "Your questions are not available" mean?
It usually means the homework was submitted too early or the submission time expired.

Popper forms:
- Popper form packets can be purchased at the University Copy Center in the Welcome Center for supported courses such as Math and Chemistry.
- Students must choose the correct popper form for their section.
- Students must fill out identifying information completely.
- Use a #2 pencil as instructed.

Can poppers be turned in at CASA?
No. They must be turned in with the class.

Can poppers be turned in to a different section?
No. They must be submitted to the section in which the student is officially enrolled.

Are some popper grades dropped?
Sometimes instructors may drop some low scores, but students should confirm with the instructor.

Online quizzes:
- Some math classes require a Course Policy Quiz.
- Students may need a perfect score to unlock other online work.
- Students may have multiple attempts depending on the class setup.

Grade posting:
- Popper grades are usually posted within three working days.
- During busy testing periods it may take up to a week.
- Test scores are often posted about one week after the testing period closes.

If a grade is missing:
- It may be a scan issue.
- Students should contact the instructor.

Grade disputes:
- Students should first speak with a CASA tutor if appropriate.
- If needed, they should contact the instructor.
- Students do not contact graders directly.

Free response test guidance:
- Show all work clearly on the official response sheet.
- Scratch paper is not used for grading.
- Free response images may be viewable in Courseware.
- Free response grading often takes 2 to 5 business days after the testing period ends.

15. PROCTORING SERVICES AND DISTANCE ASSESSMENT
==================================================

Proctoring services are for students currently enrolled in online University of Houston courses when permitted by the instructor.

Basic process:
1. Verify proctoring with the instructor.
2. Find a suitable proctor.
3. Submit a proctor request form.
4. Wait for approval email.
5. Schedule exams with the proctor.
6. Complete the exam and follow instructions afterward.

Proctor requirements:
- no relatives or friends
- no conflict of interest
- must continuously monitor the student
- must have reliable internet and email
- documentation must be in English
- exams are sent only to approved business email addresses
- acceptable locations include universities, community colleges, testing centers, or public libraries
- residential settings are not allowed
- third-party proctors like ProctorU or ProctorFree are not allowed if UH policy states they are prohibited for that process

Timing:
- domestic requests should usually be submitted at least one week before the exam
- international requests should usually be submitted at least two weeks before the exam

Nursing students:
- proctoring requests typically must be submitted two weeks before the exam date
- nursing students must use an approved proctoring location

Canceling proctoring requests:
Students may cancel by contacting the office.
Repeated no-shows may lead to cancellation of future requests.

Proctoring contact:
proctoring@uh.edu
Phone: (713) 743-5434

18. PRIVACY AND DATA
==================================================

CASA states that it may collect and process some student information, including:
- name
- PeopleSoft ID
- grades
- email address
- student pictures taken at registration

CASA states that student biometrics are hashed and stored in a secure database and cannot be extracted from the hash values.

CASA states that it uses data to:
- provide access to testing and tutoring services
- send alerts or newsletters
- gather feedback and improve services

CASA states that it does not sell or share personal information to third parties for digital marketing, advertising, or promotion of products or services.

CASA states that:
- data is stored on University of Houston-owned servers
- data is kept as long as necessary for the purpose collected or as legally required
- persistent cookies are not stored on user machines
- session cookies are deleted when the user logs out
